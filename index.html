Unemployment rate 2008 - 2020

Unemployment rate is a popular topic that people discuss about the most nowadays. The International Labour Organization provided this data related to umeployment rate which refers to the share of the labor force that is without work but available for and seeking employment.

Global Unemployment Rate

In 2020, the annual unemployment rate of most Countries is less than 10%.

In most countries, the unemployment rate changed over time. Some region that near China, India, Greenland and Algeria which have lowest unemployment rate over most of the time. Also, some countries such as South Africa, Namibia, Colombia and Spain which have highest rate of unemployment over time .

Unemployment rate by Education Level 2008-2020

Overall, for all the Region, the Annual Employment Rate decreases from 2008-2020

+ East Asia & Pacific and South Asia have the highest unemployment rate in Intermiadate Education and lowest in Basic Education

+ Europe & Central Asia and Noth America have the highest unemployment rate in Basic Education and lowest in Advanced Education

+ Latin America & Caribbean and Sub-Saharan Africa have the highest unemployment rate in Intermiadate Education and lowest in Advanced Education

Unemployment rate by gender in 2008-2020

Overall, for all the region, the annual umemployment of Male is higher than Female

The annual unemployment rate in most of Region is decreasing from 2008 to 2020. In addition, in Europe & Central Asia, Latin America & Caribbean and North America, the highest unemployment rate is from 2013 to 2015

Unemployment rate in different Region

From 2008-2019, Europe & Central Asia has highest annual unemployment rate with the country Kosovo. Also, East Asia & Pacific has lowest annual unemployment rate with the country Brunei

In general, countries with high GDP per capita have low annual unemployment rates than countries with low incomes.
